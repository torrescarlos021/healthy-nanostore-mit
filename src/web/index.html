Innovation Diffusion Model

Simulating adoption patterns of healthy products using the Bass Model and Agent-Based Modeling

📊 Theoretical Framework

Based on Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations (1962) and the Bass Model (1969), we simulate how healthy products spread through nanostore networks. The model captures both external influence (marketing, promotions) and internal influence (word-of-mouth, social proof).

🎯 Key Parameters

Innovation Coefficient (p = 0.03): Probability of adoption from external influence. 3% of non-adopters will try healthy products just from seeing ads or health promoters.

Imitation Coefficient (q = 0.38): Probability of adoption from social influence. 38% is HIGH - Mexicans are strongly influenced by what neighbors and family do. This means social campaigns work better than ads. 

# Bass Diffusion Model
Adoption Rate = p(M - N) + q(N/M)(M - N)

# Calibrated Parameters
p = 0.03  # Innovation (external influence)
q = 0.38  # Imitation (social influence)
M = 1,100,824  # Total nanostores (DENUE 2024)

# Price Elasticity (from literature)
elasticity = -0.59  # 10% price drop → 5.9% more sales

Transforming Data into Social Impact

Our simulations demonstrate that targeted interventions in Mexico's 1.1 million nanostores can significantly reduce diabetes and obesity incidence. By combining public data from INEGI, ENSANUT, and CONAPO with primary survey data (104 responses) and advanced Machine Learning techniques, we provide quantitative evidence to inform public health policy.
